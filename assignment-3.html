Assignment 3

This page is desgined to demonstrate and explain the usage of CSS, mainly in regards to styling text. The text formatting on the other parts of this site is fairly basic due to the lack of any substantial content, so I decided to make this page specifically to show off text styling.

Most of the text on this page is just to fill out the page and demonstrate the styling. While the text does describe what I did, it can all be gleamed from just looking at the css here.

Menus

The menus at the top of this page are done entirely through CSS. Instead of using a text underline to highlight each selection when hovered over, I used a border on the bottom that seemed more fitting of a menu.

All of the options in the menus are also all links, so I had to do a bit of work to get them to not look or feel at all like default links.

Links

For example, check out this link to the Wikipedia article on Malbolge. The text is only underlined when hovered over, like how it was discussed in class. They're also bolded because I think that looks nice. However, there are also other links (like the headers on the projects page) that don't look anything like regular links. I also made the :visited pseudo-class on the links not change their color.

Headers

The headers at the top of each page are <h1> tags. The underline is actually just a drop shadow. The headers on the pages for assignments and for projects are just linked styled to look like headers because they needed to actually go somewhere. The headers on this page are the exact same, but they aren't links so I just used <h2> tags instead.

Other

There's a lot of other small things that are also subtly styled using CSS. For example, the footer's a bit smaller and centered. Paragraphs inside of headed blocks (the things on this page) get indents, as they should be read more like paragraphs. The font is hopefully Helvetica, unless you don't have it. In that case, it's Arial.
